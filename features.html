Features

A whole day, held lightly.

Sempa is built around four calm rituals — plan, focus, reflect, connect — and a hundred small, well-made pieces that make them feel effortless. Here's what's inside.

New · Local AI assist
Plan

Start with intention, not a list.

Plan Day is a guided morning ritual: write your intention, see what carried over from yesterday, and pull tasks from your backlog into today.

The Week board is a Kanban across seven days — drag tasks between days and statuses, and set the handful of Weekly Objectives that actually matter.

  • Daily intention + carry-over from yesterday
  • Drag tasks across days and backlog → planned → in progress → done
  • A single Backlog as your inbox of ideas

Focus

Protect the work that matters.

Give a task a scheduled start and end and it appears as a time-block beside your calendar. Open it in Focus mode to work distraction-free with a built-in Pomodoro.

Completed sessions are logged per task, so you can see time actually spent against your estimate — gentle feedback, not surveillance.

  • Time-boxing onto the Schedule panel
  • Pomodoro with per-task session history
  • A soft “roughly at” hint when you don't want a hard block

Reflect

Close the day cleanly.

The Shutdown ritual walks you through ticking off what's done, rescheduling what isn't, and recording a win. The Weekly review captures wins, challenges, and your next focus.

It all collects in the Journal — a quiet timeline of intentions and reflections you can search and revisit.

  • Guided end-of-day shutdown with reschedule
  • Weekly review: wins · challenges · next focus
  • Optional contextual reflections inline on day & week screens

Connect

Your email and calendar, quietly pulled in.

Star a Gmail or Fastmail message and it becomes a task. See Google, Fastmail, CalDAV and ICS calendar events beside your plan. Sync assigned Jira issues — marking one done closes the ticket.

Messy subject lines are tidied into clean task titles by a local AI model running on your own server — no email leaves your infrastructure and no API key is needed.

Per-task reminders fire by Web Push, native Android, or a webhook (ntfy, Gotify), with ten calm alert tones. Everything optional, configured after login.

  • Email → tasks (Gmail, Fastmail, forwarding inbox)
  • On-device AI title cleanup — fully local, no third party
  • Calendars: Google · Fastmail · CalDAV · ICS feeds
  • Jira two-way sync · recurring task templates

Widgets

Your day, a glance away.

Keep today in view without opening the app. A floating desktop widget stays quietly above your work — always on top, a small live list of what's next.

On Android, home-screen widgets come in three sizes and tick tasks off in place — no app launch required.

  • Floating desktop widget — always-on-top, today at a glance
  • Android home-screen — 2×2 progress · 4×2 task list · 4×4 week
  • Tap a task to complete it right from the widget

Local AI · entirely optional

Help that asks first —
and never leaves your server.

An optional assistant runs on a small language model you host yourself. It only ever suggests — you approve everything — and not a byte is ever sent to a third party. Off by default; turn on only what you want.

Break into subtasks

The favourite: a checklist from one line.

Hand a big, vague task the ✦ spark and Sempa proposes a few concrete, ordered steps. Add them all, edit them, or dismiss them — the original task is never touched unless you choose to.

Because it runs on your own model, there's no API key, no account and no per-use cost — and if the model isn't running, the spark simply doesn't appear.

  • Suggest-only — nothing changes until you approve it
  • Ordered, concrete steps you can keep or rewrite
  • Fully local · works offline · no third party

Optional

Off by default. Nothing runs until you switch it on — during install or in Settings — and each feature has its own toggle.

Private

No data ever leaves your infrastructure. No third parties, no API keys, no telemetry — ever.

Local

Runs on a small model (Ollama) you host yourself — no usage costs, no rate limits, works offline.

Seven more ways it can help — each one optional.

Import a recipe or article

Give it a link or paste some text and it becomes a task with ordered steps as sub-tasks and a checklist of items — into an editable preview before anything is saved. Recipes are read directly; the rest goes through your local model.

Natural-language quick add

Type “lunch with Sam thu 1pm 30m #personal” and it fills in the title, date, time, estimate and tags.

Suggest tags

Recommends tags drawn only from your existing set, so your taxonomy stays clean and consistent.

Title cleanup & summary

Tidies imported email and Jira subjects into concise, action-oriented titles with a rough estimate.

Plan my day

Suggests a focused order for today's tasks around your calendar events — applied only when you click.

Draft weekly review

Drafts your wins, challenges and next focus from the week's completed work — you edit before saving.

Reflection prompts

Offers a couple of context-aware end-of-day questions based on what you did and didn't finish.

All can be turned off

One switch each. Or one switch for everything.

Every feature above is an independent toggle in Settings → Integrations → AI. Turn off the ones you don't want and they disappear from the app entirely; turn the whole assistant off and Sempa behaves exactly as it did before.

You also manage the model yourself — see what's downloaded and its size, switch models, pull a new one with a live progress bar, or remove one to reclaim disk. The default is small and CPU-friendly.

All changes synced

At home on your phone

Swipe a task left to push it to tomorrow, long-press the app icon for New task / Plan day / Shutdown, and share a link or text from any app straight into Sempa as a task.

Recurring, timezone-aware

Daily, weekly and monthly templates that roll over on your home timezone. Task times float, so they never shift when you travel — and Sempa offers to adjust when it notices you're away.

For the keyboard-driven

Move without the mouse.

A command palette jumps you anywhere and runs any action — press ⌘K (or CtrlK), start typing, and hit enter. Anything it doesn't recognise falls through to search.
